  • Metasuit Sensational Realities

    We are eight mechanical engineering students at ETH Zurich, working with the Soft Robotics Lab and Sensory-Motor Systems Lab, aiming to revolutionise VR/AR experiences by applying the novel HASEL technology in unseen and impactful ways.


    Stage 1

    Research and develop self-sensing human-like HASEL muscle prototypes.

    Stage 2

    Integrate sensor into soft textiles and read data into virtual environment.

    Stage 3

    Map movement of the entire human body precisely into VR and test applications.
  • Our Vision

    MetaSuit envisions the integration of soft artificial muscles into a full-body suit which allows to map the pose and movement of people into a VR/AR space. The projectâ€™s main objective is to create a soft, light and natural feeling suit that will allow users to move and interact within VR environments and overcome many of the drawbacks of vision based motion capture systems and handheld devices.

  • Technology Status Quo

    Current VR peripherals are often vision-based (cf. image) and quite expensive. The drawbacks of these systems are that they only function properly in good lighting conditions and stop working entirely once the device is out of the visible range. Further, these systems often rely on bulky actuators or input devices, which can hinder free motion of the user. For this reason, we are currently researching knowledge about HASEL actuators, so we can later implement this technology into our suit.

    Innovation

    For MetaSuit, we will be using Hydraulically Amplified Self-healing Electrostatic (HASEL) actuators (cf. videos). Our team is working with several experts and researchers in the field of soft and self-sensing actuators. This combined knowledge allows us to design and create our envisioned suit, which can then be used in various other applications. The innovation we create will spark further innovation in the industry.
